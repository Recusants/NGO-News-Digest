About NGO News Digest

Organisational Profile

The NGO News Digest is a Zimbabwe-based news and media initiative dedicated to sharing, amplifying, and celebrating the work of non-governmental organisations (NGOs) operating across Zimbabwe. The platform highlights the impact, activities, partnerships, and outcomes of civil society work in key development sectors such as agriculture, livelihoods, food security, water, health, sanitation (WASH), education, economic empowerment, protection, and community resilience.

It serves as a communications bridge between NGOs, development partners, government stakeholders, and the broader public, helping tell the stories of change and support behind NGO programmes in Zimbabwe. Its content includes success stories, photos, videos, and news about NGO efforts.

Vision

To be Zimbabwe's leading platform for celebrating and amplifying civil society impact, where stories of humanitarian action, development innovation, and community transformation inspire collaboration and collective progress toward universal access to basic life services.

Mission

To document, share, and elevate the work of NGOs in Zimbabwe by:

  • Providing timely, accurate, and engaging coverage of NGO initiatives.
  • Showcasing how NGO interventions contribute to national development priorities.
  • Connecting donors, partners, and communities through impactful storytelling.
  • Strengthening public understanding and appreciation of civil society's role in national resilience and service delivery.

Core Objectives

  1. Highlight Impact: To systematically profile NGO programmes that tangibly improve lives in sectors such as health, education, food security, WASH, livelihoods, and protection.
  2. Promote Collaboration: To foster stronger partnerships between NGOs, government entities, donors, and communities by sharing insights into who is doing what and where.
  3. Support Transparency: To increase accountability and understanding of resource flow and programme outcomes in Zimbabwe's civil society space.
  4. Champion Civil Society: To advocate for the enabling environment and recognition of NGOs as essential partners in achieving sustainable development goals and complementing government service delivery.

Principles

NGO News Digest operates with the following guiding principles:

  • Integrity and Accuracy — Ensuring that all shared stories and news are truthful, verified, and respectfully presented.
  • Inclusivity — Representing a wide range of NGOs and community voices across Zimbabwe's diverse regions and sectors.
  • Impact-Focus — Prioritising content that reflects real changes and outcomes for communities.
  • Collaboration — Encouraging cooperation between civil society actors, government, communities, and development partners.
  • Accessibility — Sharing information in formats that are widely accessible to civil society stakeholders and the general public.

What Makes NGO News Digest Unique

NGO News Digest stands out in Zimbabwe's development landscape because it explicitly focuses on telling the stories behind NGO work rather than merely reporting news. It helps fill a critical information gap by:

  • Documenting grassroots impact that might otherwise go unnoticed.
  • Showing linkages between NGO programmes and national development goals, especially in areas where government services are stretched or limited.
  • Serving as a repository of positive change that NGOs are catalysing across rural and urban Zimbabwe.
  • Bringing NGO successes to a broader audience, including citizens, partners, and donors, thus enhancing civil society visibility and appreciation.

Through its storytelling, NGO News Digest not only informs but also connects stakeholders, strengthens civil society's voice, and supports collective efforts toward ensuring that every Zimbabwean has access to basic life services from clean water and health care to education and livelihoods.

Our Contributors

NGO News Digest works with experienced development professionals and journalists who bring deep insights into Zimbabwe's civil society landscape.

Tawanda Makawa

Regional Development Communications Officer, UNESCO

Tawanda Makawa is a seasoned development and communications expert currently serving as Regional Development Communications Officer for UNESCO, covering Southern Africa. He is a regular contributor to NGO News Digest, providing informed analysis and commentary on climate change, agriculture, humanitarian action, livelihoods, and business trends as they unfold within the NGO and development fraternity.

He writes in his independent capacity, drawing on more than 15 years of experience in the consultancy sector and over 10 years in development and humanitarian programming. His professional background spans work with leading international and regional organisations, including World Vision, CARE International, ECODIT (a USAID contractor), and the United Nations system.

Tawanda brings a strong blend of strategic communications, development policy insight, and field-level understanding, enabling him to contextualise NGO interventions and their contribution to national and regional development priorities.

Dr. Chipo Moyo

Development Policy Analyst

Dr. Chipo Moyo is a development policy analyst with over 12 years of experience in monitoring and evaluation of NGO programs across Southern Africa. She holds a PhD in Development Studies from the University of Zimbabwe and contributes regularly to NGO News Digest with analytical pieces on program impact, sustainability, and policy alignment.

Her expertise includes food security programming, gender mainstreaming in development projects, and community-based monitoring systems. She has worked with multiple international NGOs and donors including DFID, USAID, and the African Development Bank.

Takunda Murewa

Field Correspondent

Takunda Murewa is a field correspondent for NGO News Digest, covering grassroots initiatives and community impact stories across Zimbabwe's rural areas. With a background in community development and journalism, Takunda brings authentic voices from the field, documenting how NGO interventions are transforming lives at the community level.

He specializes in documenting success stories in WASH, agricultural livelihoods, and education programs. His work ensures that the voices of beneficiaries and local community members are represented in development storytelling.
